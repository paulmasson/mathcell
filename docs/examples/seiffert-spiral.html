If one moves on the surface of a sphere with constant speed and constant angular velocity relative to a longitudinal diameter, the result is a Seiffert spiral:

The spiral is parametrized in cylindrical coordinates by

\[ r = \operatorname{sn}(s|m) \hspace{5em} \phi = m^2 s \hspace{5em} z = \operatorname{cn}(s|m) \]

where the elliptic parameter m is employed rather than the traditional elliptic modulus. The square of this parameter sets the angular velocity.
